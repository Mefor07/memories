Kenneth's Life

KENNETH KWATEI KOJO QUARTEY was born on February 13th,1961 at Korle Bu Hospital, Accra to the late Sydney Bedford Kwatei Quartey and Theodora Alberta Quartey (nee Nicholas). He was the first born of three boys of his parents. Kenneth began his elementary education at Ghana International School in 1966. In 1971 the family moved to the US for one year for his father to obtain a Master’s degree in Preventive Veterinary Medicine. On their return to Ghana in 1972, Kenneth continued his elementary school education at Ridge Church School where he sat for his Common Entrance. He entered Achimota Secondary School in 1973 and completed form five in 1978, obtaining his “O” Levels Certificate. During his time at Achimota Secondary School, Kenneth was an all-round sportsman and played sports for his house, Guggisberg House. In 1979 Kenneth left Ghana for the United States to study Animal Science at the University of Nebraska in Lincoln, Nebraska. He graduated from the University of Nebraska in 1983 with a Bachelor’s Degree in Animal Science. At a time when most young Ghanaians were looking to leave Ghana, Kenneth returned from the United States at the end of 1983. He joined his father, Dr. Sydney Quartey, to run Sydals Farms initially as the Farm Manager and then in 1985 as the Managing Director. Sydals Farm was a poultry farm that also bred pigs, cattle, horses and other livestock. As with everything that he did, Kenneth managed Sydals Farms with passion and dedication. He oversaw the computerisation, modernisation and mechanisation of the farm with great efficiency. Because of his dedication to excellence and quality, Sydals eggs were highly sought after, especially for their distinctive yellow yolk. Sydals had a devoted clientele of mainly women for the distribution of its eggs.
